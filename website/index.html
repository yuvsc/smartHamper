SmartHamper

Are you tired of shifting through your entire family’s laundry to find your clothes? Or finding your favorite shirt in your roommate’s closet? With SmartHamper, these are problems of the past!

Motivation

Motivation

SmartHamper is a great way to divide laundry among family members or roommates. The system identifies the owner of a given piece of clothing, maps it to its owner, and automatically sorts it into the appropriate hamper. So say goodbye to struggling to identify clothing, or finding your shirts in your roommates hamper!

Technical Components

  • Raspberry Pi 3
  • IR Sensor (HC-SR04 Ultrasonic Sensor)
  • 2 x Servo Motors
  • USB Web Camera

Prototype

The prototype consisted of the IR Sensor measuring distance. When the distance is less than 100, the camera takes a snapshot of the piece of clothing displayed by the user. The snapshot gets transmitted to the Google Cloud Vision API from the Pi and returns labels of the image as well as RGB values. A vector gets created in the form of , where L is a label found matching t-shirt, shirt, sleeve, font, or text. R, G, and B correspond to the most common RGB value in the image. The vector gets sent from the Pi to the server. Then the trained SVM uses the created vector to predict which piece of clothing this item is. Once the piece of clothing is identified, it is matched with its' owner using the MongoDb entries. The server then returns the class number of the owner to the Pi, and the Pi turns its motors accordingly to move the piece of clothing to the correct location.

Results

SVM was able to determine eight different pieces of clothing. This included two pants, four unlabeled shirts, and 2 labeled shirts.

The SVM had an accuracy score of 78% using cross-validation.

Server running on EC2 instance was able to send the Pi the correct bin to put the piece of clothing in.
